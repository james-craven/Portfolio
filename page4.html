Thank You For Viewing My Portfolio!

It took some hard work to put this website together. Between formatting the seperate pages and getting github to cooperate, I got lost in the joys of figuring out puzzles and making things work. I hope you've enjoyed exploring it all as much as I have had making it. The following is more details on the individual websites as well as the resources I used to create the site:

Page 1 is a review assignment I completed that taught me how layouts work in HTML5 and CSS. Among the various principles I learned were floats, positions, and display properties in CSS. One web design that was frequently stressed was backwards compatibility/progressive enhancement.

Page 2 is an earlier review assignment from the course focusing on user experience aspects of CSS such as font styles and appealing features. Among the things I learned while completing this assignment was using non-standard font faces.

Page 3 is all about tables and their styles. I had to take a little time on this one and really study out the concepts to understand the structure and layout of a table. I learned how to combine columns and rows, apply styles to groups of columns/rows, and add borders to the table.

Once again, thank you for viewing my portfolio. I hope this has given you a good understanding of my programming ability up to this point!
